About The Challenge

Welcome to the Oman Uni Tech Challenge, an exciting opportunity for Omani university students to showcase their innovation and problem-solving skills on a global platform.


Organized by the Google Developer Students Club (GDSC) Sohar, this challenge brings together students, industry professionals, and academic leaders to address real-world problems through technology and collaboration.

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals of the United Nations

In 2015, the United Nations created 17 Sustainable Development Goals and aimed to achieve them by 2030. All 193 United Nations Member States agreed on these 17 goals to end poverty, ensure prosperity, and protect the planet.

For the 2024 Solution Challenge, your mission is to create a project that contributes to solving one or more of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals using Google technologies.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Undergraduate students (18+ years).
  • Students from the collaborating universities (Sohar University, Arabic Open University, and UTAS Shinas).
  • Students from all faculties are eligible.

Guidelines

  • Teams must consist of a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 4 members.
  • Participants can choose their team members
  • Once registered as a group, teams cannot add a new member and can only remove one member.
  • Student must register to be participant in this challenge.

Idea Submission

  • Participants must submit project ideas before starting development.
  • Ideas must be original, with no prior submissions.
  • Submitted ideas require approval for participation.
  • Submission includes problem description, proposed solution, and expected impact.

Project Submission

  • Projects must be submitted by the deadline by the group leader.
  • Submissions include a project report, code repository, and presentation/demo video.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Oman Unis Tech Challenge 2024?

    The Oman Unis Tech Challenge 2024 is an innovative competition aimed at fostering collaboration, creativity, and problem-solving among undergraduate students from various universities in Oman. Organized by the Google Developers Students Club at Sohar University, in collaboration with Arabic Open University and UTAS Shinas, this challenge encourages students to develop tech solutions that address real-world problems and contribute to the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

  • Who can participate?

    Undergraduate students aged 18 and above from all faculties of Sohar University, Arabic Open University, and UTAS Shinas are eligible to participate.

  • How do I register?

    You can register for the challenge through the provided registration form. Make sure all team members' information is included if you are registering as a group.

  • What if I don't have a team?

    If you don't have a team, you can still register individually. We will help you form a team with other participants.

  • What are the team formation rules?

    Teams must consist of 2 to 4 members. Once registered as a group, teams cannot add new members and can only remove one member.

  • What should be included in the idea submission?

    Your idea submission should include a brief description of the problem being addressed, the proposed solution, and the expected impact. The idea must be original and not previously submitted in other challenges or competitions.

  • What happens after the idea is submitted?

    After submitting your idea, it will be reviewed for approval. Once approved, you can start working on your project and submit it by the specified deadline.

  • What is required for the final project submission?

    The final project submission should include a project report, a code repository, and a presentation/demo video. More detailed requirements can be found in the "Project Submission Guidelines" document.

  • Are there any workshops or resources available?

    Yes, we will be hosting a series of workshops to help you build your solution. These workshops will cover various topics such as web development, database integration, user experience design, and more.

  • How can I get more information or ask further questions?

    If you have any questions or need more information, please contact Khalsa Said, GDSC Lead at khalsasaidart@gmail.com. You can also reach out via our Instagram account: @gdsc_soharuni.
